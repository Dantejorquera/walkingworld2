Istambul

This places are waiting for you!

  • Hagia Sophia (Ayasofya) – A stunning architectural marvel, originally a Byzantine church, later a mosque, and now a museum (though it has been reconverted into a mosque). Famous for its massive dome, intricate mosaics, and rich history.
  • Blue Mosque (Sultanahmet Camii) – Known for its striking blue İznik tiles and six minarets, this 17th-century mosque is an iconic symbol of Istanbul.
  • Topkapi Palace (Topkapı Sarayı) – The former residence of Ottoman sultans, featuring lavish courtyards, the Harem, and treasures like the Prophet Muhammad's relics.
  • Grand Bazaar (Kapalıçarşı) – One of the world's oldest and largest covered markets, with over 4,000 shops selling jewelry, carpets, spices, and souvenirs.
  • Basilica Cistern (Yerebatan Sarnıcı) – A mysterious underground water reservoir from the Byzantine era, featuring Medusa-head columns and atmospheric lighting.

Athens

This ancient history hope for you visit!

  • Acropolis of Athens – The iconic ancient citadel perched on a rocky hill, featuring the Parthenon, Erechtheion, and breathtaking views of the city. A UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Acropolis Museum – A modern museum housing artifacts from the Acropolis, including sculptures, friezes, and the original Caryatids.
  • Ancient Agora of Athens – The heart of classical Athens, home to the well-preserved Temple of Hephaestus and Stoa of Attalos, a reconstructed ancient shopping arcade.
  • Plaka Neighborhood – Athens' oldest district, known for its charming narrow streets, neoclassical houses, souvenir shops, and traditional tavernas.
  • Temple of Olympian Zeus (Olympieion) – One of the largest temples in the ancient world, with towering Corinthian columns dedicated to Zeus.

Napoli

Discover the vibrant life for the skinny streets

  • Pompeii Archaeological Park – The legendary Roman city buried by Mount Vesuvius in 79 AD, featuring preserved ruins, frescoes, and plaster casts of victims. A UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Naples National Archaeological Museum (MANN) – Houses one of the world's finest collections of Greco-Roman artifacts, including treasures from Pompeii and Herculaneum.
  • Historic Center of Naples (Centro Storico) – A vibrant, UNESCO-listed district with narrow alleys, Baroque churches (like Naples Cathedral), and the famous Via San Gregorio Armeno (Christmas Alley).
  • Castel dell'Ovo – Naples' oldest seaside castle, perched on a tiny island in the Gulf of Naples, offering panoramic views and linked to ancient legends.
  • Royal Palace of Naples (Palazzo Reale) – A 17th-century palace showcasing opulent royal apartments, a grand staircase, and the Teatro di San Carlo, Italy's oldest opera house.
